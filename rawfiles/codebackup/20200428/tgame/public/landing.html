The Trajectory Pool game can be fairly played by both human and game bots. With random errors added to each shot, strategy and optimization become much more important than aiming.

Game bot is a great project for anyone interested in learning to code, from beginner to intermerdiate levels, especially students from 6 to 12 grades.

You can follow our tutorials to write the JavaScript code for a game bot that plays the Trajectory Pool game, and then use this game bot to compete with other players' bots in our online tournament.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why should I play in the tournament?
Top players are awarded with national leaderboard recognition and cash awards up to $1000 (depending on number of participants).

In addition, after each tournament, we will assign a new rating to your game bot based on its performance. As you enhance your bot and play in more tournaments, your bot's rating should rise over time, serving as a measurement of how much improvement you are making.
How to register?
You need to first sign up for a free account and then click the 'Tournament' menu link on top to register for this tournament. To learn to build your game bot, you can follow the tutorials under the 'Robot' menu link.
How much money does it cost?
There is no cost. You can sign up, take tutorials and compete in the tournament all for free.
How much time does it take to build my bot?
To build a starting game bot, you only need to complete the 12 beginner tutorials, which takes about 10 to 20 minutes each.

To make your bot smarter and unique, you need to take the more open-ended intermediate level tutorials. Usually it takes 20 to 30 minutes to complete each intermediate lesson, but the more time you invest to explore the open challenges, the stronger your bot will be.
What if I don't know how to code?
No problem. Our tutorials teach you how to create the game robot program in JavaScript from scratch. Prior knowledge in programing is helpful but not required.
Do I play in both regional and national levels?
All players will be playing in regional tournament on October 29, and the top players from each region will be invited to play in the national level on October 30.

The regional sections will be determined based on the number of players from each region (city or state) to ensure there are not too many players in a section. You will find out your assigned section from our tournament page one day before the regional tournament starts.
What's the tournament like?

If you have ever seen a chess tournament, this is very similar, except it is all done automatically online.

All that you need to do is to register for the tournament. At the tournament day, our game server will pair up all participants' game bots and run the match game on your behalf.

You will be able to view your bot's pairing and match results online.

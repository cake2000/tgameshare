Lesson A-1: Saving Coco

[Unplugged, Preparation]


Overview

In this lesson, students learn the basics of giving directions as a program through unplugged activities. The motivating context is that 1/3 of pets go missing, and we can use technology to help locate them and bring them home.

Students will first learn to draw arrows on the paper map to send the puppy Coco home, and then represent the directions as a program of symbols.


Materials

Worksheets


New Blocks

none


New Vocabulary

  • Command - An instruction that tells someone or a device (e.g., computer) to do something
  • Program - A sequence of text or symbols representing steps of actions


Teaching Guide

Slide 1: Saving Coco (2 minutes)

Before slide:

  • Introduce yourself, and ask students to introduce themselves if they don't know each other;
  • Explain this course is about working with computer to do fun games and solve problems;
  • Explain that there will be an AI teacher to help you throughout the course to present animations and evaluate student work;

After slide:

  • That's a lovely dog! The name "Coco" is cute!
  • Why does Coco need to be saved? Let's find out!

Slide 2: 1 out of 3 Missing (2 minutes)

After slide:

  • That is so sad! Do you know anyone who have lost his/her pet?
  • So any idea how we can help them?

Slide 3: 1 out of 3 Missing (2 minutes)

After slide:

  • Who has heard of microchips? Explain each chip is unique, and the chip can be injected under a pet's skin.
  • Explain a drone is just a small robot airplane, which can be controlled by human or a computer.

Slide 4: Saving Coco (2 minutes)

After slide:

  • Reinforce the 4 directions' names: up, down, left, right.

Slide 5: Sending Coco Home (3 minutes)

During pause:

  • Ask students to discuss with neighbors.
  • ask some student to give the direction.

After slide:

  • Explain the process again on the screen: Coco will be in the middle box after following the first left arrow, and then he'll be home after taking the second left arrow.

Slide 6: Another Example (3 minutes)

During pause:

  • Ask students to discuss with neighbors.
  • Ask some student to give the direction.

After slide:

  • Explain the process again on the screen: Coco will be where after each step.

Slide 7: Activity - Worksheet 1 (6 minutes)

During pause:

  • hand out worksheet 1 to students

After slide:

  • Give students a minute to fix incorrect answers.

Slide 8: Writing a Program (2 minutes)

After slide:

  • Explain again the program has the exact same set of arrow, except they are listed in one row instead of on the map.

Slide 9: Another Example (2 minutes)

During pause:

  • Ask students to discuss with neighbors.
  • Ask some student to answer.

After slide:

  • Reinforce that the program has the same arrows as the map.

Slide 10: Activity - Worksheet 2 (5 minutes)

During pause:

  • Hand out worksheet 2 to students

After slide:

  • Give students a minute to fix incorrect answers.

Slide 11: Reading a Program (2 minutes)

During pause:

  • Pick a student to give the answer.

After slide:

  • Use hand to illustrate how Coco moves after each arrow, and how each arrow from the program is mapped to the arrows in the map .

Slide 12: Another Example (3 minutes)

During pause:

  • Ask students to discuss with neighbors.
  • Pick a student to give their group's answer.

After slide:

  • Use hand to illustrate how Coco moves after each arrow, and how each arrow from the program is mapped to the arrows in the map .

Slide 13: Activity - Worksheet 3 (5 minutes)

During pause:

  • Hand out worksheet 3 to students

After slide:

  • Give students a minute to fix incorrect answers.

(Optional) Slide 14: Activity - Worksheet 4 (4 to 8 minutes)

After slide:

  • Explain how to play again if there is any question.
  • Hand out worksheet 4 to students
  • Ask students to read out their answers for each question.

Slide 15: Wrap Up (1 minute)
